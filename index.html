Accessibility

I am interested in many aspects of accessibility. I enjoy considering ways to serve and help individuals with diverse needs, ensuring that technology and everyday experiences are inclusive for everyone.

  • Visually Disabled – Exploring ways to improve screen readers, contrast settings, and tactile feedback for better interaction.
  • Cognitively Challenged – Thinking about how to simplify user interfaces, reduce cognitive load, and improve clarity in instructions.
  • Mobility-Impaired Users – Investigating accessible navigation, voice controls, and alternative input methods to aid usability.
  • Hearing-Impaired Individuals – Supporting captions, sign language recognition, and text-based communication alternatives.

My consideration of accessibility comes from a hope to ensure that no one is left behind when seeing my digital content or physical spaces. I am particularly interested in how modern AI and software solutions can bridge these gaps, making technology more intuitive and inclusive.
